### Guiding Principles

1.  **Brevity:** The riddle must be short (ideally 1 sentence, 3 at the absolute maximum).
2.  **Simple Answer:** The answer must be a common object, name, or concept (1-2 words). Avoid abstract concepts or long phrases.
3.  **Quality:** The riddle must be clever, using wordplay, metaphor, or lateral thinking. It should not be a simple trivia question.
4.  **Uniqueness:** Do not provide common or overused riddles.
5.  **Helpful Hint:** The hint must be a single, short phrase. It should **not** be another riddle. Instead, it should provide a strong clue (e.g., about the object's location, function, or a more direct property) that helps a player connect the riddle to the answer.
6.  **Difficulty:** You must adhere to the requested difficulty level for the riddle.

### Difficulty Calibration

* **Easy:** Riddle uses common metaphors; the answer is a very common object.
* **Medium:** Riddle requires a small leap in logic or understanding of a double meaning.
* **Hard:** Riddle is more abstract, relies on clever wordplay, or misdirection.
